## Soil Preparation: The Foundation You Can't Skip

This is where 90% of DIY sod jobs fail. The grass is only as healthy as the soil underneath it. Professional preparation involves:

- **Soil Testing:** We analyze pH levels and nutrient composition. Jacksonville's sandy soil is often acidic and nutrient-poor. Without amendments, even the best sod will struggle.
- **Grading:** Proper grading ensures water drains away from your home's foundation. An amateur grade can send water pooling against your house, causing thousands in water damage.
- **Debris Removal:** Rocks, old roots, construction debris, and compacted soil must be removed. A thin layer of sod over rocky soil will never root properly.
- **Amendment Application:** Based on soil tests, we add lime, compost, or specific nutrients to give the sod the best possible start.

## The "Fit" Matters: Professional Cutting and Laying Techniques

Laying sod is like laying tile. The edges, the seams, and the pattern all matter.

- **Staggered Seams:** Like brickwork, sod pieces should be offset to prevent long seam lines that dry out and separate. DIYers often lay them in a grid, creating weak points.
- **Tight Butt Joints:** Each piece must be pressed firmly against the next without overlapping or leaving gaps. Gaps dry out and become weed highways. Overlaps create bumps and uneven surfaces.
- **Proper Cutting:** Around sprinkler heads, tree roots, walkways, and curves, sod must be custom-cut with a sharp knife. Tearing or forcing pieces leads to visible seams and poor root-to-soil contact.
- **Rolling:** After installation, a heavy lawn roller is used to press the sod firmly onto the prepared soil, eliminating air pockets that prevent rooting.

## Timing, Watering, and the First 14 Days

Even perfectly laid sod can fail without the right aftercare plan. This is the critical establishment period.

**Professional watering schedules** are calibrated to the season, the grass type, and the specific conditions of your yard. A general rule is to water 2-3 times daily for the first 7-10 days, keeping the soil consistently moist (not waterlogged). After that, we transition to deeper, less frequent watering to encourage root growth.

We provide every customer with a detailed, written aftercare guide specific to their grass type and the time of year. This isn't generic advice from a YouTube video; it's a plan based on decades of local experience.
